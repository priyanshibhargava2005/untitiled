Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture. Known as the "Pink City" due to its distinct pink-colored buildings, Jaipur offers a plethora of attractions:​

Amber Fort: A majestic fort located on a hilltop, showcasing a blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture. ​

Hawa Mahal: Also known as the "Palace of Winds," this five-story structure features 953 small windows, allowing royal ladies to observe street festivals without being seen. ​
Tripadvisor

City Palace: A complex of courtyards, gardens, and buildings, reflecting a fusion of Rajput and Mughal styles. ​

Jantar Mantar: An astronomical observatory housing the world's largest stone sundial, showcasing the scientific advancements of the 18th century. ​
Tripadvisor

Jaigarh and Nahargarh Forts: Offering panoramic views of the city and housing historical artifacts. ​

Birla Mandir: A modern white marble temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi, known for its intricate carvings. ​
Tripadvisor

Jaipur's bustling bazaars, such as Johari and Bapu Bazaar, offer traditional handicrafts, jewelry, and textiles. The city's vibrant festivals, including the Elephant Festival and Teej, provide a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage.​
